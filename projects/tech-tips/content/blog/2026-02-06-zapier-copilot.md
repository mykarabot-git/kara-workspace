## How Copilot Changes the Game

Instead of manual point-and-click workflow building, Copilot lets you describe your automation in plain English—and it handles the rest.

Type or say something like: *"When a new lead comes through Typeform, enrich it with LinkedIn data, add it to HubSpot, and send a Slack notification to the sales team."*

Copilot:
- Selects the right apps and triggers
- Configures field mappings automatically
- Adds appropriate steps for data transformation
- Sets up authentication where needed
- Even suggests filters, paths, and other logic you might want

The AI version doesn't just save minutes—it saves context switching, reduces setup errors, and lets you focus on *what* you want to automate, not *how* to build it step by step.

## Zapier Agents vs. Zaps: Know When to Use Each

Before we dig into Copilot, let's clarify the difference between Zaps and Agents—they serve different purposes:

**Zaps** are precise, predictable automations. You define every step explicitly. Use Zaps when:
- You need exact control over each action
- The workflow follows a consistent pattern
- You're integrating known apps with structured data

**Zapier Agents** are AI-powered assistants that make decisions based on your instructions. Use Agents when:
- You need the AI to research, summarize, or analyze
- The workflow has open-ended or variable steps
- You want the AI to choose which apps/steps to use

Copilot builds both types. It's like having an automation Expert who asks clarifying questions, builds drafts, and lets you iterate until it's perfect.

## Real Example: Building a Lead Management Workflow

Let me show you exactly how fast Copilot makes this.

**Scenario:** We need a system that captures leads from a website form, researches company information, scores the lead, and notifies the appropriate sales rep.

### Step 1: Start with Copilot

Open Zapier and click the Copilot icon (anywhere in the platform). Then type:

```
Build a lead management workflow that captures leads from a web form, enriches them with company data from the web, scores them based on company size and industry, and routes them to the right sales rep with a Slack notification.
```

### Step 2: Let Copilot Build

Copilot analyzes your request and starts constructing the workflow. It might ask a few clarification questions (like which apps you use), then create a draft Zap.

The draft includes:
- A Typeform or Google Forms trigger (depending on your answer)
- A step to look up company data using web search or Crunchbase/BClearbit
- A Formatter step to score the lead based on criteria
- A Path step to route different lead tiers to different reps
- A Slack notification with lead details

### Step 3: Iterate and Refine

See a step that could be better? Type a follow-up:

```
Actually, use Clearbit for company data instead, and add a 15-minute delay before the Slack notification so reps can review in HubSpot first.
```

Copilot updates the workflow immediately—replaces the data lookup step, adds the delay, and adjusts the notification.

### Step 4: Test and Publish

Click Test to verify everything works. Copy any sample data you need, then Publish.

**Time saved:** Instead of manually configuring 5-6 steps across multiple apps, adjusting field mappings, and debugging, you spent 3-5 minutes in a natural conversation.
